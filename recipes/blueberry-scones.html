Blueberry Scones with Lemon Glaze

Description

This recipe from Food Network is one that I come back to every year after blueberry picking in late June. It's a hit with my family, and I'm sure it'll be a hit with you as well!

Ingredients

Blueberry Scones:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, cold, cut in chunks
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup heavy cream, plus more for brushing

Lemon Glaze:

  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cups confectioners' sugar, sifted
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 lemon, zest finely grated

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Sift together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Fold in the blueberries, being careful not to mash the berries.
  3. Make a well in the center of the batter and pour in the heavy cream. Fold just to incorporate; do not overwork the dough.
  4. Press the dough into a 12 x 3 inch rectangle. Cut into 4 squares parallel to the short side of the rectangle. Then, cut those squares in half diagonally to form triangles. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet, brush with heavy cream, and bake for 15 to 20 minutes until golden brown.
  5. While the scones are baking, prepare the lemon glaze. Melt the butter in a double-boiler, then whisk in the lemon juice and zest. Add the confectioners' sugar about 1/4 cup at a time and whisk until dissolved.
  6. Once the scones have finished baking, drizzle the glaze over top. Let rest for a few minutes, then serve warm.

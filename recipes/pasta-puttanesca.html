Pasta Puttanesca

Description

I first tried this lovely, salty dish at Sirianni's Café in Davis, WV. I was a bit afraid to try anchovies and capers for the first time, but it was definitely worth it! This particular recipe comes from Budget Bytes and serves 5.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 anchovy filets
  • 1 28oz. can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper
  • 1/2 cup sliced kalamata olives
  • 2 tablespoons capers
  • 1 lb spaghetti

Steps

  1. Add the olive oil, onion, garlic, and anchovy filets to a large skillet and sauté over medium heat until the onions are soft and the anchovies have dissolved.
  2. Add to the skillet the can of crushed tomatoes, basil, crushed red pepper, sliced olives, and capers. Stir to combine, then allow to come up to a simmer. Turn the heat down low and let simmer, stirring occasionally.
  3. Boil a large pot of water over high heat and add the spaghetti. Boil about 7 minutes, then reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta water before draining.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the saice in the skillet. Stir to combine, then serve hot.
